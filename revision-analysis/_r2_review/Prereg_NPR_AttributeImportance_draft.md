1) Have any data been collected for this study already?

No, no data have been collected for this study yet.

2) What's the main question being asked or hypothesis being tested in this study?

When participants imagine selecting expert sources as an NPR producer, the self-reported importance of considering the percentage of experts who (1) were under 50 years old, (2) were based on the West Coast of the United States, or (3) worked at a university is equally or more important than the percentage of experts who were women.

3) Describe the key dependent variable(s) specifying how they will be measured.

The primary DV is a single 7-point importance rating (1 = "Not at all important", 7 = "Very important").

4) How many and which conditions will participants be assigned to?

Participants will be randomly assigned with equal probability to 1 of 4 between-subjects conditions. The conditions are defined by which expert attribute participants are asked to self-report the level of importance of: the percentage of experts who 1) were women, 2) were under 50 years old, 3) were based on the West Coast of the United States, or 4) worked at a university.

5) Specify exactly which analyses you will conduct to examine the main question/hypothesis.

We will conduct three two-sided t-tests on the 7-point importance rating, each comparing the Gender condition (reference) against one of the three comparison conditions:

- Gender vs. Age (under 50 years old)
- Gender vs. Geography (West Coast)
- Gender vs. Occupation (university)

Because a non-significant two-sided t-test alone cannot establish that two means are equivalent, we will additionally conduct three one-sided non-inferiority tests using the two one-sided tests (TOST) procedure (Schuirmann, 1987; Lakens, 2017; Lakens, Scheel, & Isager, 2018) to directly support the "equally" portion of our prediction. For each Gender-vs-comparison contrast, the null hypothesis is that the comparison attribute is rated more than 0.4 SD less important than Gender; rejecting this null (one-sided p < .05) supports the claim that the comparison attribute is at least as important as Gender within a pre-specified smallest-effect-size-of-interest (SESOI) of d = 0.4. We selected d = 0.4 because it corresponds to a difference of roughly 0.7 points on the 7-point importance scale (assuming pooled SD ≈ 1.7), the boundary of what we consider a substantively meaningful gap. With approximately 75 participants per cell, the non-inferiority test has approximately 80% power at α = .05.

6) Describe exactly how outliers will be defined and handled, and your precise rule(s) for excluding observations.

We will exclude all participants who exit the survey before being assigned to an experimental condition.

7) How many observations will be collected or what will determine sample size?

We will recruit 300 US-based, English speaking participants from Prolific with 90% approval rate and at least 1,000 completions.
