# Codex brief v3: rewrite Section 3 opener — fix causal-logic bug

## What changed from v2

JC reviewed the v2 draft and flagged a logical inconsistency. The v2 draft is:

> Increasing the representation of women and racial minorities in workplaces, leadership, and other high-visibility roles remains a salient concern for organizations and the publics they serve (Devine & Ash, 2022; Bentley University & Gallup, 2025), even as some organizations have expanded and others have scaled back their public commitments to DEI (Levi & Fried, 2024; Heaton, 2025; Elias & Palmer, 2025). **Several organizations have responded by giving decision makers regular feedback** on the demographics of those they have previously chosen to spotlight, hire, or promote (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC), in the hope that such feedback will spur more inclusive subsequent selections. Whether purely descriptive feedback of this kind can be an effective intervention remains an open empirical question.

The phrase "have responded by giving decision makers regular feedback" implies a causal / temporal chain: rhetoric fluctuated → organizations responded with descriptive-feedback programs. That is factually wrong. NPR has been logging source demographics since 2013, the BBC's 50:50 Project since 2017, Salesforce's diversity scorecards since 2020. Descriptive-feedback practices PREDATE the post-2024 DEI pullback by years. Presenting them as a "response" to that pullback misrepresents the timeline AND undercuts the timelessness argument JC is building (the practice's relevance does not depend on the rhetoric being in any particular state).

## What v3 needs to do

1. Acknowledge fluctuation in DEI rhetoric (sentence 1 stays close to v2).
2. Introduce the descriptive-feedback practice as an EXISTING, ONGOING practice — NOT as a response to recent rhetoric shifts. The practice and the rhetoric shifts should sit side by side without implied causal relationship.
3. Ideally, gently convey that the practice has been around for years (without dates or specific firm-by-firm start years, which would re-timestamp the paragraph).
4. Preserve the rest of the arc: practice → open empirical question.

## Phrasing options to consider (verb choice for the practice)

- "Several organizations (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC) **have for years given** decision makers regular feedback…" — captures duration without dates
- "Several organizations (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC) **provide** decision makers with regular feedback…" — simple present, neutral, no duration claim
- "**One practice that some organizations have employed for years** is to give decision makers regular feedback…" — frames the practice as longstanding, structurally decouples from the rhetoric shift
- "At several organizations (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC), **decision makers receive regular feedback**…" — passive-voice reframing, just describes the ongoing state of affairs

Pick the verb framing you find cleanest and most consistent with JC's voice (compare with manuscript_gdoc_text.txt line 9 abstract paragraph and surrounding intro prose for register matching).
