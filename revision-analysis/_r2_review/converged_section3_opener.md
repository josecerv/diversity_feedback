# Converged rewrite: Section 3 opener (Descriptive Feedback about Diversity-Related Decisions)

## Recommended (3 sentences)

> Although the corporate posture toward DEI has fluctuated over time and across regions, the question of who gets selected, hired, promoted, and featured remains consequential for organizations and the people they serve (Levi & Fried, 2024; Bentley University & Gallup, 2025). Some organizations have responded by giving decision makers regular feedback on the demographics of those they have previously chosen to spotlight, hire, or promote (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC), in the hope that such feedback will spur more inclusive subsequent selections. Whether purely descriptive feedback about past selections can be an effective intervention remains an open empirical question, and one that arguably grows in practical importance as organizations become more reluctant to issue explicit diversity goals.

1. **Removes pre-2024 framing.** The old opener (“many companies are making sustained investments… eager for new tools…”) overstated current corporate commitment. The new opener acknowledges that the corporate posture has fluctuated without naming years, surveys, or firms.
2. **Modernizes citations.** Out: Kirkland & Bohnet (2017), SHRM (2022), Richard (2000), Hsieh et al. (2019), Jehn et al. (1999). In: Levi & Fried (2024) on fluctuating DEI rhetoric and Bentley University & Gallup (2025) on continued public support — both already in the R2 reference list.
3. **Reinforces timelessness.** The closing clause (“arguably grows in practical importance as organizations become more reluctant to issue explicit diversity goals”) is the response letter’s exact argument compressed to a single beat, and it lands inside this paragraph rather than getting deferred to paragraph 3.
4. **Preserves original arc.** (a) motivation, (b) focal practice with NPR / Salesforce / BBC anchors, (c) open empirical question. The next paragraph (line 25) does the heavier durable-forces work (anti-discrimination law, international transparency directives, etc.), so this opener stays light.
