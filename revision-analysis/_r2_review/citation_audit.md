## Citation-by-citation findings

### 1. Locke, E. A., & Latham, G. P. (2019). *Motivation Science*, 5(2), 93-105. [10.1037/mot0000127]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed (scite + Crossref).
- Use: cited for the goal-setting/feedback claim. Aligned with the paper's goal-setting theory retrospective.
- **Action**: KEEP AS IS.

### 2. Itzchakov, G., & Latham, G. P. (2018 or 2020). *Applied Psychology*, 69(2), 379-414. [10.1111/apps.12176]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed. Crossref shows online publication 2018-12-12; print issue April 2020.
- Use: cited for feedback-plus-goals motivating behavior change. Aligned with the abstract.
- **Action**: FLAG. Year ambiguity. APA 7th allows the issue date (2020), but online-first dates (2018) are also commonly cited. Response letter uses 2018; manuscript uses 2018. Defensible either way. Recommend keeping 2018 for consistency with the response letter unless JC prefers strict APA 7th issue-year style.

### 3. Chen, X., Latham, G. P., Piccolo, R. F., & Itzchakov, G. (2020). *Applied Psychology*, 70(1), 216-253. [10.1111/apps.12239]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed via Crossref. **Note: paper has 4 authors, not 3.** scite returns only the first 3; Codex flagged the missing 4th author and Crossref confirmed.
- Year: Crossref's official year is 2020 (online first), but the paginated issue is January 2021. Either is defensible; we kept 2020 since the response letter uses 2020.
- Use: cited for primed goal effects supporting goal-setting theory. Aligned with the abstract.
- **Action**: FIX. Reference-list entry needs Itzchakov as the 4th author. Body citations stay as "Chen et al., 2020" under APA 7th convention (3+ authors → first author + et al.).

### 4. Schultz, P. W., Nolan, J. M., Cialdini, R. B., Goldstein, N. J., & Griskevicius, V. (2018). *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 13(2), 249-254. [10.1177/1745691617693325]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed via Crossref. **Note: paper has 5 authors, not 3.** scite returns only the first 3; Codex flagged the missing 4th and 5th authors; Crossref confirmed all 5.
- Use: cited for the 2007/2018 Schultz reprise on descriptive feedback + normative messaging.
- **Action**: FIX. Reference-list entry needs all 5 authors. Body citations should be "Schultz et al. (2018)" or "Schultz et al., 2018" per APA 7th (the response letter's "Schultz, Nolan, and Cialdini (2018)" is too short for a 5-author paper).

### 5. Andor, M. A., Gerster, A., Peters, J., & Schmidt, C. M. (2020). *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*, 103, 102351. [10.1016/j.jeem.2020.102351]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed via Crossref. **Note: paper has 4 authors, not 3.** scite returns only the first 3; Codex flagged the missing 4th author; Crossref confirmed Schmidt.
- Claim alignment caveat (both passes flagged): the paper's actual finding is that home-energy-report (HER) effect sizes are *much lower* outside the US ("our evidence from a large-scale randomized controlled trial in Germany suggests that effect sizes are actually much lower than in the US"). Citing this as a pattern "echoed" in conservation interventions overstates its supportive role.
- **Action**: FIX (two parts). (a) Add Schmidt to the reference-list entry. (b) Soften the Item 2 wording from "a pattern echoed in more recent applied interventions" to a more neutral framing such as "a pattern that has been further tested across applied interventions in energy and household conservation, with effect sizes that vary by national context" so the Andor citation is not misrepresented.

### 6. Henry, M. L., Ferraro, P. J., & Kontoleon, A. (2019). *Energy Policy*, 132, 1256-1261. [10.1016/j.enpol.2019.06.039]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Use: cited for an applied feedback intervention in household electricity. Aligned with the abstract (2.9% reduction in a randomized field trial).
- **Action**: KEEP AS IS.

### 7. Mertens, S. N., & Schultz, P. W. (2021). *Journal of Environmental Psychology*, 73, 101541. [10.1016/j.jenvp.2020.101541]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Use: cited for applied feedback in residential recycling. Aligned with the abstract.
- **Action**: KEEP AS IS.

### 8. Devine, P. G., & Ash, T. L. (2022). *Annual Review of Psychology*, 73(1), 403-429. [10.1146/annurev-psych-060221-122215]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Use: cited in Item 8 for documenting the expansion of DEI training programs and infrastructure.
- **Alignment note**: this review is *critical* of diversity-training efficacy ("the enthusiasm for, and monetary investment in, diversity training has outpaced the available evidence that such programs are effective in achieving their goals"). The Item 8 use is for documenting the boom (which the paper does describe), not for vouching for DT efficacy. JC should know this when reading reviewer feedback.
- **Action**: KEEP AS IS. The issue-number question Codex flagged ("(1)" vs not) is minor; APA 7th includes issue numbers when available, so keeping "73(1)" is defensible.

### 9. Levi, A., & Fried, Y. (2024 or 2025). *Journal of Organizational Behavior*, 46(1), 172-187. [10.1002/job.2834]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Year ambiguity: Crossref lists 2024 (online publication); the paginated issue is volume 46(1), 2025. Either year is defensible.
- Use: cited in Item 6 for the argument that DEI rhetoric is a downstream signal of broader pressures, not the source of the implicit injunctive norm. The paper's abstract explicitly supports this framing ("organizations have responded to powerful societal forces" → "emphasize the symbolic aspects of diversity").
- **Action**: FLAG. Keep 2024 for consistency with the response letter; switch to 2025 if JC prefers strict issue-year APA style.

### 10. Ressia, S. (2024). *Journal of Industrial Relations*, 66(5), 742-758. [10.1177/00221856241295490]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Use: cited in Item 8 for documenting the Australian WGEA Amendment (Closing the Gender Pay Gap) Act 2023. The article explicitly reviews this amendment.
- **Action**: KEEP AS IS.

### 11. Mignano, V. (2024). *Zeitschrift für Europarechtliche Studien*, 27(3), 371-401. [10.5771/1435-439X-2024-3-371]
- Existence + metadata: confirmed.
- Use: cited in Item 8 for the EU Pay Transparency Directive 2023/970 AND for the June 2026 member-state transposition deadline. The paper's analysis of the Directive is well-aligned, BUT the June 2026 deadline is explicitly a feature of the Directive itself (Article 34 of Directive (EU) 2023/970), not a finding of Mignano's analysis.
- **Action**: FIX. Keep Mignano (2024) as the analytical commentary citation, AND add the Directive itself as the source for the June 2026 transposition deadline.
