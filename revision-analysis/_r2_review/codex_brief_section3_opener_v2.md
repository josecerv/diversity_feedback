JC reviewed the v1 converged draft and flagged a problem with the closing clause "arguably grows in practical importance as organizations become more reluctant to issue explicit diversity goals." That clause tethers the paragraph too tightly to the CURRENT MOMENT (post-2024 DEI pullback). The whole point of the timelessness fix is to avoid timestamping in EITHER direction:

- not in the pre-2024 sustained-investment direction (the original opener's problem)
- not in the post-2024 "more relevant now because firms are retreating" direction (v1's problem)

JC's exact framing: "we are hesitant of feeling too timestamped in either direction (either feeling dated or too tethered to the current moment)... The goal is that we make a broader point about relevance despite changing tides in recent years. So ideally we sort of both acknowledge these tides and proclaim the importance regardless of external tidal shifts."

JC also reminded us to watch placement: the deeper "durable forces" argument (anti-discrimination law, international transparency directives, individuals' own egalitarian commitments) is already developed in the third paragraph of this section (line 25 of `manuscript_gdoc_text.txt`). ## Why this matters

The paragraph's job in the section is narrow: motivate why we study this, name the focal practice, pose the empirical question. The timelessness argument LIVES in paragraph 3 of the section (which already does substantial work on anti-discrimination law, international transparency, individuals' own commitments). If the opener crowds into that territory, paragraph 3 becomes redundant. If the opener picks a side of the current moment, R2's "timestamped" critique returns.

The ideal opener probably reads as if it could have been written in 2018 or 2026 — it acknowledges that corporate rhetoric is not stable, asserts the question matters anyway, and gets out of the way.
