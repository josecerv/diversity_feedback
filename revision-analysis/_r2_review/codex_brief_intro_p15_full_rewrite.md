The earlier brief (`codex_brief_intro_dei_paragraph.md`) asked for a 2-4 sentence replacement of just the closing sentence of Intro ¶15. ## Current state of ¶15 (verbatim from manuscript)

> In many societies worldwide, it is considered unacceptable to exhibit racial or gender prejudice, with legal frameworks and organizational policies supporting diversity across numerous countries (e.g., the UK Equality Act 2010; EU Council Directive 2000/43/EC; Civil Rights Act of 1964, Álvarez-Benjumea, 2023). In the United States, for example, outward expressions of prejudice are both a societally sanctioned norm violation (Álvarez-Benjumea, 2023) and a violation of the law, which prohibits acts of discrimination based on gender or race (Civil Rights Act of 1964). Similarly, in the European Union, the Racial Equality Directive explicitly prohibits racial discrimination in the labor market, codifying the region's strong normative commitment to equal treatment regardless of racial or ethnic origin (Council Directive 2000/43/EC). Moreover, many organizations have explicitly committed to increasing the representation of women and racial minorities (among other groups) within their ranks (Ellis, 2025; Devine & Ash, 2022). And despite recent rollbacks of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) policies at some organizations in the United States (Heaton, 2025), recent surveys indicate that the majority of Americans still hold favorable views about DEI (Minkin, 2024; Kidwai, 2025).

## What the rewrite needs to do

1. Keep the first 3 sentences (intro, US Civil Rights Act, EU Racial Equality Directive) essentially as they are — these are the load-bearing normative-structure claims.
2. **Add** a new sentence (or two) framing recent international advances as *continuing expansion of the normative/regulatory structure* — Australia's WGEA 2023, EU Pay Transparency Directive 2023/970, EU Women on Boards Directive 2022/2381. Cite Ressia (2024) and Mignano (2024). These reinforce the global commitment story; they are NOT framed as reactive or fluctuating.
3. Keep the "Moreover, many organizations have explicitly committed…" sentence (Ellis, 2025; Devine & Ash, 2022) intact.
4. **Replace** the final "And despite recent rollbacks…" sentence with a more measured treatment that:
   - Acknowledges the US corporate posture toward DEI has fluctuated.
   - Argues firms' pullbacks reduce only one source of external pressure.
   - Lists the structural pressures that continue: anti-discrimination law, the visibility of selection decisions to colleagues / candidates / supervisors / customers / other stakeholders, employee and stakeholder expectations, and individuals' own egalitarian commitments.
   - Closes with the scope claim: descriptive feedback should be most likely to matter when past selection patterns raise concerns about prejudice, less likely in environments where those concerns are absent, muted, or explicitly rejected.
