Although organizations’ diversity-related rhetoric and decision-making practices fluctuate (Levi & Fried, 2024), managers still routinely make choices about whom to spotlight, hire, and promote, and those choices can produce selection patterns that descriptive feedback makes visible. Providing regular feedback to employees about the demographics of whom they have previously selected is an approach some organizations, including NPR, Salesforce, and the BBC, are adopting in the hope that such feedback will prompt decision makers to select more diverse candidates without requiring an explicit diversity goal. Yet it remains an open empirical question whether purely descriptive feedback, by making the demographic composition of prior decisions salient, can be an effective intervention for changing subsequent selection decisions.
