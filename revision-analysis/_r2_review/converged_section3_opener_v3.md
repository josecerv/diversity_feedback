# Converged rewrite v3: Section 3 opener — logic bug fixed

## The bug in v2

> ...even as some organizations have expanded and others have scaled back their public commitments to DEI (Levi & Fried, 2024; Heaton, 2025; Elias & Palmer, 2025). **Several organizations have responded by giving** decision makers regular feedback...

"Have responded by" implies the descriptive-feedback practice was a reaction to recent DEI-rhetoric shifts. That is factually wrong:

- NPR has logged source demographics since **2013**
- BBC 50:50 Project since **2017**
- Salesforce diversity scorecards since **2020**

All three predate the post-2024 pullback. The practice is independent of (and predates) the rhetoric fluctuation. Presenting it as a response misrepresents the timeline AND weakens the timelessness argument — the practice's relevance doesn't depend on the rhetoric being in any particular state.

## Proposed replacement v3 (recommended)

> Increasing the representation of women and racial minorities in workplaces, leadership, and other high-visibility roles remains a salient concern for organizations and the publics they serve (Devine & Ash, 2022; Bentley University & Gallup, 2025), even as some organizations have expanded and others have scaled back their public commitments to DEI (Levi & Fried, 2024; Heaton, 2025; Elias & Palmer, 2025). For years, several organizations (e.g., NPR, Salesforce, the BBC) have given decision makers regular feedback on the demographics of those they have previously chosen to spotlight, hire, or promote, in the hope that such feedback will spur more inclusive subsequent selections. Whether purely descriptive feedback of this kind can be an effective intervention remains an open empirical question.

## What "For years" does

- **Decouples the practice from the rhetoric shift.** "Have responded by" implied causation; "For years… have given" explicitly establishes that the practice predates and exists independently of the recent rhetoric fluctuations.
- **No dates, no firm-by-firm year markers.** Avoids re-timestamping. The fact of duration ("for years") is asserted without anchoring to 2013 / 2017 / 2020.
- **Sentence-initial placement does the work loudly.** Putting "For years" at the front of sentence 2 makes the temporal decoupling the first thing the reader takes in after the fluctuation framing.
- **Defensible empirically.** NPR ~13 years, BBC ~9 years, Salesforce ~6 years as of 2026. "For years" fits all three.

## What I changed from Codex v3

- **Active voice.** Codex used "decision makers have for years received" (passive). Switched to "several organizations… have given decision makers regular feedback" — matches the abstract's active framing of the same idea ("Many organizations provide employees with feedback…").
- **Tense consistency.** Codex used "those they previously chose" (simple past). Switched to "those they have previously chosen" — present perfect throughout, and matches the existing manuscript's phrasing exactly (line 21: "whom they have previously chosen").
- **Adverb placement.** Codex's "have for years received" is grammatical but the adverb sandwiched between auxiliary and main verb is slightly heavy. Moving "For years" to sentence-initial position lets it do its decoupling work without breaking up the verb phrase.
