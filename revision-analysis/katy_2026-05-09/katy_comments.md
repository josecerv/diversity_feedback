# Cross-cutting principles (Katy's red lines)

1. **Replication-grade detail must be preserved somewhere.** Demographics,
   inclusion thresholds, procedural display details, baseline-balance
   stats. If repeated, footnote on first appearance ("unless otherwise
   stated…").
2. **Detailed write-up appears once in Study 1; later studies may
   condense.** George wanted *later* studies streamlined, not Study 1.
3. **DVs must be named at every report.** Don't replace the noun with a
   pronoun or pure number.
4. **All key stats must appear somewhere** (main text or appendix), with
   "all p's > X" allowed as a main-text shorthand only when an appendix
   carries the full numbers.
5. **Original wording often beats rewrite.** Where the prior phrasing was
   clearer, revert.
