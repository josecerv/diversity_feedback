## BEFORE
Study 3A

Methods
Participants. As pre-registered (https://osf.io/4z8hk), we recruited 1,000 participants on Amazon’s MTurk. Participants were based in the U.S., had a minimum 90% approval rate and at least 1,000 prior completions, and were paid $0.75 to complete a 3-minute survey (46.1% men; 51.4% women; Mage = 42.20; 72.3% White, 12.0% Black, 6.2% Hispanic, 8.8% Asian).
Procedure. Participants were informed about a real campaign planned on Facebook to advertise a set of popular and inspirational biographical films (“biopics”), and they were truthfully informed that they would be weighing in on which films to include in the advertisement. Participants were provided with a list of 25 candidate films for inclusion in the ad. Above each film name, we included a thumbnail of its cover art and a brief description listing the following information: the name of the film’s protagonist (e.g., Judy Garland, Alan Turing), a description of the protagonist’s claim to fame (e.g., Actress, WWII codebreaker), the film’s year of release (e.g., 2019, 2014), and the budget for the film (e.g., $10m, $14m). Of these 25 films, 4 (16%) featured a protagonist who was a racial minority. 
Participants began by selecting a portfolio of seven films for the Facebook campaign, and they were informed that they would be incentivized for the quality of their decisions. Specifically, we communicated the following statement to participants: “To provide extra motivation to take this seriously, we’ll send you a $5 bonus if your list is selected (there will be an eventual vote on all of the lists generated).” After data collection, we followed this procedure and chose one participant’s curated list of seven films to advertise on Facebook; that participant received a $5 bonus.
After making their initial seven film selections, participants were randomly assigned to either (1) the no race feedback or (2) the race feedback condition. In the no race feedback condition, participants were presented with three pieces of descriptive feedback about the films they had selected: the percentage that (1) had a budget above $40M, (2) were released after 2010, and (3) were over 2 hours long. In the race feedback condition, participants always received descriptive feedback about the percentage of films they had selected that featured a racial minority protagonist alongside two randomly drawn pieces of feedback from the list of film attributes summarized in the no race feedback condition. After receiving descriptive feedback on the composition of their initial film selections, participants selected an eighth and final film for the Facebook campaign from the original set, where their prior selections were highlighted. See Materials Section: Study 3A in the Appendix for complete study materials.
Results
Prior to random assignment, participants in both conditions selected an initial portfolio of seven films. Of these initial selections, 16.1% featured racial minority protagonists (a statistic that was balanced across conditions: 16.6% in the no race feedback condition and 15.8% in the race feedback condition, t(998) = 1.01, p = .314, 95% CI = [-0.007, 0.023]). Following our pre-registration, we ran an OLS regression with robust standard errors to predict whether the eighth and final biopic selected featured a racial minority protagonist. Our primary predictor was an indicator for assignment to the race feedback condition. As shown in Figure S2A and Model 1 of Table S3, we found that participants were significantly more likely to choose a biopic featuring a racial minority protagonist for their eighth and final biopic in the race feedback condition (27.10% chose a biopic featuring a racial minority protagonist) than in the no race feedback condition (17.73% chose a biopic featuring a racial minority protagonist; B = 0.094, 95% CI = [0.042, 0.145], t(998) = 3.57, p < .001).
As shown in Figure S2A and Table S3 (Models 5-8), and as predicted, the effect of assignment to the race feedback condition on the likelihood of selecting an 8th and final film with a racial minority protagonist was larger than the effect of assignment to receive descriptive feedback about the percentage of initial films selected with a (1) budget above $40M (F(1, 1992) = 7.71, p = .006), (2) release year after 2010 (F(1, 1992) = 13.74, p < .001) or (3) average duration of over two hours (F(1, 1992) = 6.92, p = .009) on subsequent selections of an 8th and final biopic with, respectively, a (1) budget above $40M, (2) release year after 2010 or (3) average duration of over two hours.
